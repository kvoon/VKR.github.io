COVID-19 Deadliness EDA

This project was an individual component of a university group assignment, wherein each member was tasked with conducting an individual analysis of COVID data to ascertain the "Deadliness of COVID-19." Every member was tasked with selecting a particular country and a variable for investigation, examining how it influenced the deadliness of COVID-19 in the chosen nation. In my case, I choose Malaysia and opted to investigate the Stringency Index as the variable of interest. The stringency index quantifies the strictness of government policies, in response to crises like the COVID-19 pandemic.

The Process Notebook encompasses Data Engineering, EDA processes, and custom functions, including two for generating frequently used graphs to enhance code reusability and another for populating a Pandas data frame with random values. The latter is utilised to test the reliability of reused functions in the broader Product Notebook, promoting modularity.

Note: the findings and values presented in the written analysis may differ from the generated graphs and tables due to daily updates in the dataset during the creation of the Jupyter Notebook. Therefore, the values and findings present in the written analysis only accurately represent the data as it stood during the fourth quarter (Q4) of 2020.

Cafe Sales Predictor

This project used an SQLite dataset with transaction data from a pop-up cafe at the University of Sydney's Camperdown campus. The cafe owners wanted to answer two main questions with this project:

  1. Identify the factors that influence the average hourly sales at the cafe.
  2. Select an appropriate statistical model and investigate the feasibility of creating a predictive model to forecast average hourly sales based on the available dataset.

The project encompasses a Jupyter Notebook and an associated project report, which comprehensively documents the analysis and model development journey. It also includes solutions to both of these questions, providing a detailed account of the findings and methodologies used.

Walkability Analysis

The project was undertaken originally as part of a Data Science university group project, done as a 2-person team.

The objective of this assignment waas to develop a walkability score for Sydney neighborhoods based on factors like density, destinations, distance to transport, and additional data like cafes, cycling infrastructure, public transport connectivity, and greenery. Analyze the correlation between walkability scores, median income, and average monthly rent.
